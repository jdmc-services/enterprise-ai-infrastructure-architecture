## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the governance framework for data used in enterprise AI systems, including data classification, access control, retention, and quality requirements for AI training data, retrieval knowledge bases, and AI-generated outputs.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all enterprise AI systems, including but not limited to: retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) assistants, AI-powered analytics tools, automation workflows incorporating AI decision-making, and any system that processes enterprise data using AI or machine learning models.

## 3. Data Classification Requirements

All data used in enterprise AI systems must be classified according to the Enterprise Data Classification Policy before use. Tier 4 (Restricted) data is prohibited from use in AI systems without explicit CISO and Legal approval and a documented risk acceptance.

## 4. Knowledge Base Governance

Documents ingested into AI knowledge bases must:
- Be approved by a named governance owner prior to ingestion
- Carry a valid classification tier assignment
- Include complete metadata (owner, version, effective date, review date)
- Be reviewed annually for continued accuracy and relevance

## 5. AI Output Governance

AI-generated outputs must:
- Be treated as advisory — not authoritative — unless validated by a human subject matter expert
- Include citations to source documents when based on retrieved content
- Not be used as the sole basis for decisions affecting patient care, regulatory compliance, or significant financial commitments

## 6. Retention and Deletion

AI knowledge base content follows the same retention schedule as the source documents. When a source document is retired, its embedded representation must be removed from the AI knowledge base within 30 days.
